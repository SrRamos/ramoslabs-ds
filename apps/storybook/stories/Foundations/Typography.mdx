<div className="typo-section">
  <h2 className="typo-section-title">Weight, Line Height and Tracking</h2>
  <p className="typo-section-lede">
    These three axes carry hierarchy without adding a single new size. Used well they let one family cover Display down
    to Label. Used carelessly they are the fastest way to break legibility.
  </p>

  <div className="typo-rule-card">
    <div className="typo-rule-title">Weight: eight steps, a working range of five</div>
    <div className="typo-rule-body">
      The scale runs <code>--font-weight-thin</code> (100) to <code>--font-weight-extrabold</code> (800). In practice
      the product lives in five: <code>normal</code> (400) for body, <code>medium</code> (500) for labels and buttons,
      <code>semibold</code> (600) for titles, <code>bold</code> (700) for headlines, and <code>extrabold</code> (800)
      reserved for large Display. The three light weights (<code>thin</code>, <code>extralight</code>, <code>light</code>)
      are display-only. Never set body or any text at or below 16px in a light weight: thin strokes lower the effective
      contrast against the background and fail readers with low vision.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="typo-rule-card">
    <div className="typo-rule-title">Line height: inverse to size</div>
    <div className="typo-rule-body">
      Big text needs less leading, small text needs more. Display and Headline use <code>--leading-none</code> (1) to
      <code>--leading-tight</code> (1.1) so multi-line titles hold together. Titles use <code>--leading-snug</code>
      (1.25). Body must use at least <code>--leading-normal</code> (1.5), because WCAG 1.4.12 requires a line-height of
      at least 1.5 times the font size within paragraphs; long-form copy moves to <code>--leading-relaxed</code> (1.625)
      for easier line tracking. Setting a paragraph tighter than 1.5 is an accessibility regression, not a style choice.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="typo-rule-card">
    <div className="typo-rule-title">Tracking: negative for big, positive for caps</div>
    <div className="typo-rule-body">
      Default body tracking is <code>--tracking-normal</code> (0em); the font is already spaced for reading, so leave it
      alone. Large Display can tighten to <code>--tracking-tight</code> (-0.025em) or <code>--tracking-tighter</code>
      (-0.05em) to close the optical gaps that open up at big sizes. Positive tracking, <code>--tracking-wide</code>
      (0.025em) and <code>--tracking-wider</code> (0.05em), is only for uppercase Labels and small caps, where extra
      space between letters restores the legibility that all-caps removes. Never track lowercase body text, positive or
      negative.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="typo-section">
  <h2 className="typo-section-title">Readability and Accessibility</h2>
  <p className="typo-section-lede">
    The scale exists so text is comfortable to read and legible to everyone, including readers who zoom, override
    spacing, or have low vision. These are the non-negotiable floors, not aspirations.
  </p>

  <div className="typo-measure">
    <span className="typo-measure-tag good">measure 45 to 75ch</span>
    <p className="typo-measure-good">
      Line length, the measure, is set by capping the container in <code>ch</code> units, not by font size. Around 66
      characters per line is the comfortable target, and anything from roughly 45 to 75 characters reads well. At this
      width the eye finds the next line without effort and the paragraph feels calm. Cap body columns with
      <code> max-width</code> in <code>ch</code>, never leave running text at full container width.
    </p>
    <span className="typo-measure-tag bad">too wide, over 75ch</span>
    <p className="typo-measure-bad">
      When a line runs much past seventy-five characters the reader has to travel too far to the start of the next line, loses their place, and rereads. Long measures are the single most common readability failure in dashboards and admin tools, where a paragraph is dropped into a wide container and simply allowed to stretch edge to edge. The words are fine. The width is the bug. This line is deliberately set to a 120ch cap so the discomfort is visible: notice how much more work it takes to get from the end of one line back to the beginning of the next compared with the constrained column above it.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div className="typo-a11y-grid">
    <div className="typo-a11y-card">
      <span className="typo-a11y-crit">WCAG 1.4.4 AA</span>
      <div className="typo-a11y-name">Resize text to 200%</div>
      <div className="typo-a11y-body">
        Text must scale to 200% without loss of content or function. This is why the scale is defined in
        <code> rem</code>, not <code>px</code>: rem-based type respects the user's browser font-size and zoom. Never
        pin a font size in px, and never lock layouts so enlarged text is clipped or overlaps.
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="typo-a11y-card">
      <span className="typo-a11y-crit">WCAG 1.4.12 AA</span>
      <div className="typo-a11y-name">Text spacing overrides</div>
      <div className="typo-a11y-body">
        Content must survive user-applied spacing: line-height 1.5x font size, paragraph spacing 2x, letter-spacing
        0.12x, word-spacing 0.16x. Use flexible containers and spacing tokens so nothing is cut off when a reader
        overrides these values.
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="typo-a11y-card">
      <span className="typo-a11y-crit">WCAG 1.4.8 AAA</span>
      <div className="typo-a11y-name">Visual presentation</div>
      <div className="typo-a11y-body">
        The AAA target we aim for on long-form: line width no wider than 80 characters, line-height at least 1.5 in
        blocks of text, and no fully justified alignment. Our 45 to 75ch measure and <code>--leading-relaxed</code>
        body already clear this bar.
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="typo-a11y-card">
      <span className="typo-a11y-crit">Typographic contrast</span>
      <div className="typo-a11y-name">Never rely on size alone</div>
      <div className="typo-a11y-body">
        Hierarchy and meaning must survive in one color. Distinguish levels with size, weight and space, and pair every
        color contrast (headings and body use tokens meeting at least 4.5:1) with a real weight or size difference, so
        the structure holds for readers who cannot perceive the color shift.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="typo-section">
  <h2 className="typo-section-title">Fluid Type</h2>
  <p className="typo-section-lede">
    Large text should not jump at breakpoints; it should flow. Instead of redefining a heading at every media query, we
    interpolate between two real scale steps with a single <code>clamp()</code>. The pattern is
    <code> clamp(MIN, PREFERRED, MAX)</code>: MIN is the scale step for small screens, MAX is the scale step for large
    screens, and the PREFERRED term is a viewport-relative expression that carries the size smoothly between them.
  </p>

  <div className="typo-decision">
    <ol>
      <li><strong>Pick two real scale steps.</strong> The min and max of any fluid value must both be legal tokens. A Display that goes from <code>--font-size-4xl</code> (36px) up to <code>--font-size-6xl</code> (60px) stays on-scale at every width, it just moves between two steps you already sanctioned.</li>
      <li><strong>Choose the min and max viewport.</strong> We anchor the small end at 375px (mobile) and the large end at 1280px (desktop). Below and above those, <code>clamp()</code> holds the min and max flat.</li>
      <li><strong>Let the tool compute the middle.</strong> The PREFERRED term is <code>base-rem + slope-vw</code>, a linear interpolation between the two anchors. Compute it with Utopia or the web.dev clamp method rather than by hand, and keep a <code>rem</code> component in the expression so the text still responds to browser zoom (protecting WCAG 1.4.4).</li>
      <li><strong>Only Display, Headline and Title go fluid.</strong> Body and Label stay fixed at their token size. Fluid body copy changes measure unpredictably and fights the 45 to 75ch rule.</li>
    </ol>
  </div>

  <div className="typo-scale" style={{ marginTop: '1.25rem' }}>
    <div className="typo-scale-item">
      <span className="typo-scale-label">Display fluid</span>
      <span className="typo-scale-role">Display</span>

    </div>
    <div className="typo-scale-item">

      <span className="typo-scale-sample" style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.8125rem', color: '#475569', whiteSpace: 'normal' }}>clamp(1.5rem, 1.19rem + 1.33vw, 2.25rem) &nbsp; 2xl to 4xl</span>
    </div>
    <div className="typo-scale-item">
      <span className="typo-scale-label">Title fluid</span>
      <span className="typo-scale-role">Title</span>
      <span className="typo-scale-sample" style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.8125rem', color: '#475569', whiteSpace: 'normal' }}>clamp(1.25rem, 1.15rem + 0.44vw, 1.5rem) &nbsp; xl to 2xl</span>

<div className="typo-section">
  <h2 className="typo-section-title">Implementation Rules</h2>
  <p className="typo-section-lede">
    The taxonomy for applying type on a new RamosLabs surface. When these are followed, every screen inherits the same
    reading experience for free.
  </p>

  <div className="typo-dodont">
    <div className="typo-do">
      <div className="typo-do-head">Do</div>
      <ul>
        <li>Assign a role (Display, Headline, Title, Body, Label) first, then take its family, weight, leading and tracking from the role table.</li>
        <li>Reference tokens: <code>var(--font-size-lg)</code>, <code>var(--leading-normal)</code>, never a raw px or a magic number.</li>
        <li>Cap running text at a 45 to 75ch measure with <code>max-width</code>.</li>
        <li>Keep heading levels strict and sequential: h1 then h2 then h3, no skipped levels, size comes from the role, not the tag.</li>
        <li>Make large headings fluid by interpolating between two real scale steps with <code>clamp()</code>.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="typo-dont">
      <div className="typo-dont-head">Don't</div>
      <ul>
        <li>Invent a size outside the ten-step scale, or nudge one with <code>font-size: 17px</code>.</li>
        <li>Set body or small text in a light weight, or track lowercase body text.</li>
        <li>Let a paragraph stretch to full container width, or set body line-height below 1.5.</li>
        <li>Pick a heading tag for its default size, or jump from h1 to h4 to get a look.</li>
        <li>Use Red Hat Display for body, or mix Roboto and Rubik in the same block.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="typo-rule-card" style={{ marginTop: '1rem' }}>
    <div className="typo-rule-title">Naming a new type token</div>
    <div className="typo-rule-body">
      Before adding one, check the decision ladder: does an existing scale step plus weight and leading already express
      it? Almost always yes. If a genuinely new token is needed, follow the existing namespaces exactly, the categorical
      prefix then a scale name: the <code>--font-size</code> namespace (t-shirt scale), <code>--font-weight</code>
      (named weights), <code>--leading</code> and <code>--tracking</code> (semantic steps). New size steps keep the
      t-shirt convention (a hypothetical <code>--font-size-7xl</code>), not a raw number. Never introduce composite
      aliases like <code>--font-primary</code> or a per-component <code>--heading-1</code>; those hide the scale and
      drift out of sync. Add the token to <code>packages/tokens</code> so it regenerates into CSS, the JSON source is
      the single source of truth, and this page reads from it.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
