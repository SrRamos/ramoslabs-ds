        track lowercase body, positive or negative.
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

  <p className="ty-intro">
    Line length decides whether a paragraph is calm or exhausting, and font size has nothing to do with it. Cap running
    text between <strong>45 and 75 characters</strong> with <code>max-width</code> in <code>ch</code>. Around 66 is the
    sweet spot.
  </p>

  <div className="ty-measure">
    <span className="ty-measure-tag">measure 45 to 75ch</span>
    <p className="ty-measure-good">
      At this width the eye finds the start of the next line without effort and the paragraph feels calm. Cap body
      columns in ch, never leave running text at full container width.
    </p>
    <span className="ty-measure-tag bad">too wide, over 75ch</span>
    <p className="ty-measure-bad">
      When a line runs much past seventy-five characters the reader has to travel too far back to the start of the next line, loses their place, and rereads. This is the single most common readability failure in dashboards and admin tools, where a paragraph is dropped into a wide container and allowed to stretch edge to edge. The words are fine. The width is the bug, and this line is set to a 120ch cap so you can feel it.
    </p>
