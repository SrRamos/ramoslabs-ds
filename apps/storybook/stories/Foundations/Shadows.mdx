<div className="shadows-section">
  <h2 className="shadows-section-title">Why Two Layers, and Why Indigo</h2>
  <p className="shadows-lede">
    A single hard shadow is a shortcut that light does not take. Real cast shadows are two things at once: a small, near, crisp contact shadow directly under the object, and a large, far, diffuse shadow that fades into the surface. Stacking one soft layer close and one soft layer far reproduces that falloff, so the surface reads as genuinely lifted rather than pasted on. This is the layered, or smooth, shadow technique popularized by Tobias Ahlin, and it is the same falloff logic Material 3 uses for its elevation shadows.
  </p>
  <p className="shadows-lede">
    The tint matters just as much. Pure black shadows sit on top of a colored brand like a stain. Tinting the shadow with the brand indigo (<code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.75rem' }}>rgba(79, 70, 229, ...)</code>) lets the shadow belong to the surface it falls on. It is the same instinct behind Material 3 surface tint, where elevation is expressed through a tint of the primary color rather than heavier ink, and behind Apple's preference for soft, minimal shadows that suggest depth without demanding it.
  </p>

<div className="shadows-section">
  <h2 className="shadows-section-title">The One Rule: Shadow Is Height, Not State</h2>
  <p className="shadows-lede">
    A shadow means one thing only: this surface is physically above another. Use it when something actually floats, a menu over the page, a modal over a scrim, a resting card over the canvas. Never reach for a shadow to signal that an element is hovered, pressed, or selected. The dated pattern of a card that grows a dramatic shadow on hover, usually paired with a <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.75rem' }}>translateY</code> lift, fakes physics the element does not have, and it collides with this system's rule against transform-based hover effects.
  </p>
  <div className="shadows-note">
    <div className="shadows-note-title">State feedback lives elsewhere</div>
    <div className="shadows-note-body">
      Hover, press, and selection are communicated with color and state layers, not elevation. See <strong>Patterns / Interactive</strong> for the state-layer model. Keep the two channels separate: color answers "what is happening to this control", shadow answers "how high does this surface sit". When they blur together, both get noisy.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

    Dark surface rising through indigo-tinted levels with no shadow at all. Height reads from lightness, not from ink.
  </p>

<div className="shadows-section">
  <h2 className="shadows-section-title">Rules of Implementation</h2>

  <div className="rules-grid">
    <div className="rules-col do">
      <div className="rules-col-title"><span className="rules-badge">+</span> Do</div>
      <ul className="rules-list">
        <li>Use shadow only for real elevation, a surface floating above another.</li>
        <li>Stay within three levels: <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>sm</code>, <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>md</code>, <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>lg</code>.</li>
        <li>Treat <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>lg</code> as the ceiling. Add a scrim, not more blur.</li>
        <li>Keep the indigo tint. Reference the tokens, never hand-roll black shadows.</li>
        <li>In dark mode, prefer a lighter, tinted surface over a heavier shadow.</li>
        <li>Reserve <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>focus</code> for <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>:focus-visible</code> only.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="rules-col dont">
      <div className="rules-col-title"><span className="rules-badge">x</span> Don't</div>
      <ul className="rules-list">
        <li>Grow a shadow on hover to fake a lift.</li>
        <li>Pair shadow changes with <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>transform</code> hover effects.</li>
        <li>Invent a fourth or fifth level for extra drama.</li>
        <li>Use pure-black or single-layer shadows.</li>
        <li>Invent an <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>xl</code>, <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>2xl</code>, or <code style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace", fontSize: '0.72rem' }}>*-hover</code> shadow. The scale stops at three plus focus.</li>
        <li>Use the focus ring as decoration.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="shadows-section">
  <h2 className="shadows-section-title">Which Level, in Four Questions</h2>

  <div className="decision">
    <div className="decision-step">
      <div className="decision-text">Is this element actually floating above another surface? If not, use <strong>no shadow</strong>. Keep it flat on the page.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="decision-step">
      <div className="decision-text">Is it a resting element, a button or a card, that just needs to separate from the background? Use <strong><code>--shadow-sm</code></strong>.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="decision-step">
      <div className="decision-text">Does it open on top of the current view and close again, a dropdown, popover, or menu? Use <strong><code>--shadow-md</code></strong>.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="decision-step">
      <div className="decision-text">Does it take over the view behind a scrim, a modal, sheet, or command palette? Use <strong><code>--shadow-lg</code></strong>, the ceiling. There is no level above it.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
