<div className="hl-section">
  <span className="hl-num">04</span>
  <h2 className="hl-h2">The two helpers that matter most</h2>
  <p className="hl-intro">
    Two exist purely to serve assistive technology. Treat them as required infrastructure, not decoration.
  </p>

  <table className="hl-table">
    <thead><tr><th>Class</th><th>Purpose</th></tr></thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr><td><code className="cls">.sr-only</code></td><td>Visually hidden, still announced by screen readers. For icon-button labels, form hints, and status that is obvious by sight but silent to the tree.</td></tr>
      <tr><td><code className="cls">.focus-ring</code></td><td>Applies the system focus treatment on <code>:focus-visible</code>, pairing with <code className="tok">var(--color-focus)</code> so keyboard users always see where they are.</td></tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>

  <div className="hl-kicker">Inside .sr-only, declaration by declaration</div>
  <p className="hl-intro">
    Visually hidden is a solved problem with a canonical recipe. The common shortcut of <code>display: none</code> is wrong for it: that also drops the element from the accessibility tree, the opposite of the goal. This hides the pixels while keeping the text for a screen reader.
  </p>

  <div className="hl-anatomy">
    <div className="hl-anatomy-card">
      <span className="hl-anatomy-decl">position: absolute</span>
      <p className="hl-anatomy-text">Pulls the element out of flow so its collapsed box disturbs nothing around it.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-anatomy-card">
      <span className="hl-anatomy-decl">width: 1px; height: 1px</span>
      <p className="hl-anatomy-text">Collapses the box to one pixel. Not zero, since a zero-sized box can be dropped from the tree.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-anatomy-card">
      <span className="hl-anatomy-decl">overflow: hidden</span>
      <p className="hl-anatomy-text">Clips the real content down to that one-pixel box so none of it paints.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-anatomy-card">
      <span className="hl-anatomy-decl">clip: rect(0, 0, 0, 0)</span>
      <p className="hl-anatomy-text">Clips the visible region to nothing, the historical companion to overflow.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-anatomy-card">
      <span className="hl-anatomy-decl">white-space: nowrap</span>
      <p className="hl-anatomy-text">Stops the collapsed text from wrapping, which could reintroduce a sliver of layout.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-anatomy-card">
      <span className="hl-anatomy-decl">margin: -1px; border: 0</span>
      <p className="hl-anatomy-text">Zeroes the box model and nudges it off-screen so nothing leaks, not a border, not a background.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

<div className="hl-section">
  <span className="hl-num">05</span>
  <h2 className="hl-h2">Which layer, in five questions</h2>
  <p className="hl-intro">
    When you are unsure which tool to reach for, walk down until one fits.
  </p>

  <div className="hl-ladder">
    <div className="hl-step">
      <div className="hl-step-text">Just need the <strong>value</strong>, inside a component's own scoped CSS? Reference the <strong>token</strong>, for example <code>var(--space-4)</code>. No utility needed.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-step">
      <div className="hl-step-text">One structural adjustment in markup, a gap, an alignment, a width? Use the matching <strong>utility</strong>.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-step">
      <div className="hl-step-text">The same three or four utilities always travel together on the same kind of element? That pattern has a name. Extract a <strong>component</strong>.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-step">
      <div className="hl-step-text">The element needs structure, states, or behavior, a card, a modal, a checkout row? It was always a <strong>component</strong>. Do not assemble it from utilities.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="hl-step">
      <div className="hl-step-text">The need is purely for assistive tech, hiding a label or showing focus? Use <strong><code>.sr-only</code></strong> or <strong><code>.focus-ring</code></strong>. Infrastructure, not styling.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
