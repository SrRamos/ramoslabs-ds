<div className="mo-section">
  <h2 className="mo-section-title">Anatomy of a Transition</h2>
  <p className="mo-lede">
    Every transition names three things: the property, a duration token, an easing token. Name the property, never <span className="mo-inline-code">transition: all</span>, which animates things you never meant to touch. Reference the tokens instead of hardcoding milliseconds, so the whole product keeps one tempo.
  </p>

  <div className="mo-anatomy">
    <div className="mo-anatomy-line">
      <span className="mo-part punc">transition:</span>
      <span className="mo-part prop">opacity</span>
      <span className="mo-part dur">var(--duration-normal)</span>
      <span className="mo-part ease">var(--easing-out)</span>
      <span className="mo-part punc">;</span>
    </div>
    <div className="mo-anatomy-legend">
      <div className="mo-legend-item prop">
        <div className="mo-legend-key">property</div>
        <div className="mo-legend-text">What changes. Prefer <span style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace" }}>transform</span> and <span style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace" }}>opacity</span>. Name it, never <span style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace" }}>all</span>.</div>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-legend-item dur">
        <div className="mo-legend-key">duration</div>
        <div className="mo-legend-text">How long. 150ms local, 200ms to enter or leave, 300ms for a takeover.</div>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-legend-item ease">
        <div className="mo-legend-key">easing</div>
        <div className="mo-legend-text">The feel. Out to enter, in to leave, in-out to move, linear to loop, spring to celebrate.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="mo-section">
  <h2 className="mo-section-title">Performance Is Part of the Contract</h2>
  <p className="mo-lede">
    A frame at 60fps is about 16ms. The browser can animate two properties, <span className="mo-inline-code">transform</span> and <span className="mo-inline-code">opacity</span>, on the compositor, off the main thread, with no layout and no repaint. Animating geometry recomputes layout every frame, and that layout thrash is the usual cause of jank. This is how the rendering pipeline works, not a preference, so the system animates transform and opacity by default.
  </p>

  <div className="mo-perf">
    <div className="mo-perf-card cheap">
      <div className="mo-perf-head"><span className="mo-symbol" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <div className="mo-perf-props">
        <span className="mo-chip">transform</span>
        <span className="mo-chip">opacity</span>
      </div>
      <p className="mo-perf-text">Handled on the compositor, off the main thread. No layout, no paint. A slide is <span style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace" }}>translateY</span>, not a change to <span style={{ fontFamily: "'SF Mono', monospace" }}>top</span>.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="mo-perf-card">
      <div className="mo-perf-head"><span className="mo-symbol" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>
      <div className="mo-perf-props">
        <span className="mo-chip">width</span>
        <span className="mo-chip">height</span>
        <span className="mo-chip">top</span>
        <span className="mo-chip">left</span>
        <span className="mo-chip">margin</span>
      </div>
      <p className="mo-perf-text">Each frame recomputes layout, then repaints. On a long list or a weak device this drops frames. Express the same motion with transform.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="mo-section">
  <h2 className="mo-section-title">Reduced Motion Is the Floor</h2>
  <p className="mo-lede">
    Some people feel physically ill from on-screen movement. The browser exposes their OS setting as <span className="mo-inline-code">prefers-reduced-motion: reduce</span>, a firm request to hold still that every animation honors. It aligns with WCAG 2.1: <strong>2.3.3</strong> asks that interaction-triggered motion can be disabled, and <strong>2.2.2</strong> covers looping motion.
  </p>
  <p className="mo-lede">
    Honoring it does not mean deleting meaning. Because information rides on color, opacity, and the final state, the interface stays legible without movement: collapse the animation, keep the destination. And keep a spinner turning slowly rather than freezing it, a stopped spinner reads as a hung app.
  </p>

  <div className="mo-code">
    <div className="mo-code-header">
      <span className="mo-code-dot red" aria-hidden="true"></span>
      <span className="mo-code-dot yellow" aria-hidden="true"></span>
      <span className="mo-code-dot green" aria-hidden="true"></span>
      <span className="mo-code-lang">CSS</span>
    </div>
    <pre dangerouslySetInnerHTML={{__html: `<span class="comment">/* Default: motion is welcome. Enter fast, settle gently. */</span>

<div className="mo-section">
  <h2 className="mo-section-title">Rules of Motion</h2>
  <p className="mo-lede">
    Each rule stands on its own: what to do, then why it holds. Read the why once and the rule stops feeling arbitrary.
  </p>

  <div className="mo-rulebook">
    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Give every animation a job: orientation, continuity, or feedback.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text">Decorative motion clarifies nothing and costs the same frame time, so it is the first thing to cut.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Animate <code>transform</code> and <code>opacity</code>.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text">The compositor carries them off the main thread with no layout or paint. Animating <code>width</code>, <code>height</code>, <code>top</code>, <code>left</code>, or <code>margin</code> recomputes layout every frame and drops frames.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Name the property, a duration token, and an easing token explicitly.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text"><code>transition: all</code> animates properties you never meant to touch, and hardcoded milliseconds drift from the shared tempo.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Match the duration to the change: <code>fast</code> for local state, <code>normal</code> to enter or leave, <code>slow</code> for a takeover.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text">Anything past <code>300ms</code> reads as sluggish, so the ceiling holds and most motion sits at 200ms.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Pick easing by job: <code>out</code> to enter, <code>in</code> to leave, <code>in-out</code> to move, <code>linear</code> to loop.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text"><code>linear</code> on anything with a start and an end feels robotic, and the wrong curve reads as lag or machinery.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Reserve <code>spring</code> for a small, discrete success, and gate it behind reduced motion.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text">An overshooting modal looks broken, and spring is comfort, not information.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="mo-rule">
      <div className="mo-rule-head">
        <span className="mo-rule-tag">Rule</span>
        <p className="mo-rule-text">Honor <code>prefers-reduced-motion</code>: collapse the movement, keep the meaning.</p>
      </div>
      <div className="mo-why">
        <span className="mo-why-tag">Why</span>
        <p className="mo-why-text">Motion alone must never carry meaning. Information rides on color, opacity, and the final state, so the interface stays legible without movement.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
