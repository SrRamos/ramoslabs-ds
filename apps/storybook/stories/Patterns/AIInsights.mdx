<div className="aic-principle">

    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Why</span> Fluent text reads as certain, so without a visible cue users over-trust a guess.</p>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-evidence">
    <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">insights</span>
    <div className="aic-evidence-body">
      <strong>Communicate the model's confidence, and account for how people read it.</strong> Google's People + AI Guidebook devotes its Explainability and Trust chapter to showing confidence honestly, warning that a poorly framed number can mislead as much as no number at all.
      <span className="aic-cite">Google PAIR, Explainability + Trust. Sources below.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-apply">
    <div className="aic-apply-title"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">insights</span> In a RamosLabs product</div>
    <p className="aic-apply-text">
      Show confidence as a stepped meter plus a word, differentiated by fill count and label, not by hue. Three levels: high, medium, limited. When confidence is low, say so and point to what would raise it.
    </p>
    <div className="aic-gen">
      <div className="aic-gen-bar">
        <span className="aic-gen-badge"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">auto_awesome</span> Generated</span>
        <span className="aic-conf" style={{ marginLeft: 'auto' }}>
          <span className="aic-conf-bars high" aria-hidden="true"><i></i><i></i><i></i></span>
          High confidence
        </span>
      </div>
      <div className="aic-gen-body">
        Projected turnout is around <strong>820 people</strong>, based on twelve comparable events. Treat it as an estimate, not a booking guarantee.
      </div>
    </div>
    <div className="aic-gen" style={{ marginTop: '0.6rem' }}>
      <div className="aic-gen-bar">
        <span className="aic-gen-badge"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">auto_awesome</span> Generated</span>
        <span className="aic-conf" style={{ marginLeft: 'auto' }}>
          <span className="aic-conf-bars low" aria-hidden="true"><i></i><i></i><i></i></span>
          Limited data
        </span>
      </div>
      <div className="aic-gen-body">
        Only two similar events on record, so this projection is rough. Add last year's numbers to sharpen it.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-line">
    <div className="aic-line-col ok">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Frame output as an estimate and show how firm it is, in words and a meter. <strong>The reader sees both the answer and how far to trust it.</strong></p>
    </div>
    <div className="aic-line-col avoid">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Stating a guess as flat fact, or a precise 87.34 percent score that implies a rigor the model does not have. <strong>False precision is just a prettier form of overconfidence.</strong></p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

{/* ============================================ */}
{/* 3. GENERATION STATES                          */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="aic-principle">
  <div className="aic-principle-head">
    <span className="aic-num">3</span>
    <h2 className="aic-principle-title">Show the Work in Progress</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="aic-lede">
    Generation takes time and does not always land. Stream the answer as it forms so the wait feels alive, and keep the user in command of it: a <strong>stop</strong> while it runs, a <strong>regenerate</strong> when it misses. A spinner that cannot be cancelled is a trap.
  </p>

  <div className="aic-rule">
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Rule</span> Stream output as it generates and give a stop control while it runs and a regenerate after it finishes.</p>
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Why</span> Visible progress keeps a slow response tolerable, and control over it keeps the user in charge.</p>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-evidence">
    <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">tune</span>
    <div className="aic-evidence-body">
      <strong>Support efficient dismissal and correction, and let people invoke and control the service.</strong> Microsoft's guidelines call for easy dismissal (G8) and correction (G9); Apple frames on-device generation as something people should be able to interrupt and rerun.
      <span className="aic-cite">Microsoft HAX G8, G9; Apple HIG, Generative AI. Sources below.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-apply">
    <div className="aic-apply-title"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">graphic_eq</span> In a RamosLabs product</div>
    <p className="aic-apply-text">
      While tokens stream, show them arriving with a caret and swap the primary action to <strong>Stop</strong>. When done, offer <strong>Regenerate</strong> and <strong>Copy</strong>. Never leave a bare, uncancellable spinner.
    </p>
    <div className="aic-gen">
      <div className="aic-gen-bar">
        <span className="aic-gen-badge"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">auto_awesome</span> Generated</span>

      <div className="aic-gen-body">
        Your event description is coming together. The headline leads with the venue, the body highlights the lineup<span className="aic-caret" aria-hidden="true"></span>
      </div>
      <div className="aic-gen-foot">
        <button className="aic-btn primary" type="button"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">stop</span> Stop</button>
        <button className="aic-btn" type="button" disabled><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">refresh</span> Regenerate</button>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-line">
    <div className="aic-line-col ok">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Stream the response, expose Stop while running, and Regenerate plus Copy when done. <strong>The user drives the generation, not the other way around.</strong></p>
    </div>
    <div className="aic-line-col avoid">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">A modal spinner that blocks the screen with no way out, or a single answer the user cannot rerun. <strong>Removing control over a slow, fallible process turns waiting into helplessness.</strong></p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

{/* ============================================ */}
{/* 4. HUMAN IN THE LOOP                          */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="aic-principle">
  <div className="aic-principle-head">
    <span className="aic-num">4</span>
    <h2 className="aic-principle-title">Keep a Human in the Loop</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="aic-lede">
    The model proposes; the person disposes. Between a generated result and any real-world effect, insert a step to <strong>review, edit, and approve</strong>. Match the friction to the stakes: a draft email can auto-fill, but sending it, spending money, or deleting data waits for a human yes, and stays reversible.
  </p>

  <div className="aic-rule">
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Rule</span> Route consequential AI output through a human review-edit-approve step, and keep the resulting action reversible.</p>
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Why</span> The person carries the consequences, so they, not the model, make the final call.</p>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-evidence">
    <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">rate_review</span>
    <div className="aic-evidence-body">
      <strong>Let people review and edit generated content before it is used, and scope autonomy to the stakes.</strong> Apple advises giving people a chance to review and revise machine output before acting on it, and Google's guidebook ties the level of automation to the cost of a mistake.
      <span className="aic-cite">Apple HIG, Machine learning and Generative AI; Google PAIR, Feedback + Control. Sources below.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-apply">
    <div className="aic-apply-title"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">rate_review</span> In a RamosLabs product</div>
    <p className="aic-apply-text">
      Present generated output as an editable draft, not a done deed. The primary action is <strong>Approve</strong>, editing is always open, and once applied the change can be undone. See <strong>Patterns / Persuasion</strong> on reversibility as a hard line.
    </p>
    <div className="aic-gen">
      <div className="aic-gen-bar">
        <span className="aic-gen-badge"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">auto_awesome</span> Generated draft</span>

      </div>
      <div className="aic-gen-body">
        Hi Marta, thanks for registering for the workshop. Here is what to bring and how to find the room.
      </div>
      <div className="aic-gen-foot">
        <button className="aic-btn primary" type="button"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check</span> Approve and send</button>
        <button className="aic-btn" type="button"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">edit</span> Edit</button>
        <button className="aic-btn" type="button"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">close</span> Discard</button>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-line">
    <div className="aic-line-col ok">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Hold consequential output at an editable review step, then let the applied result be undone. <strong>The human approves, and can always walk it back.</strong></p>
    </div>
    <div className="aic-line-col avoid">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Auto-sending, auto-charging, or auto-deleting on the model's word, with no review and no undo. <strong>Irreversible action on an unverified guess hands the user the blame for the model's mistake.</strong></p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

{/* ============================================ */}
{/* 5. ERRORS AND HALLUCINATIONS                  */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="aic-principle">
  <div className="aic-principle-head">
    <span className="aic-num">5</span>
    <h2 className="aic-principle-title">Plan for Wrong Answers</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="aic-lede">
    A model will state a falsehood with the same confidence as a fact. Design for it. <strong>Cite the source</strong> so a claim can be checked, make correction a first-class action, and when the system cannot answer well, say so plainly instead of inventing. Graceful failure beats a confident fabrication.
  </p>

  <div className="aic-rule">
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Rule</span> Attach checkable sources to factual claims, offer a correction path, and degrade to an honest "I do not know" over a guess.</p>
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Why</span> Models hallucinate fluently, so users need a way to verify, fix, and recognize the system's limits.</p>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-evidence">
    <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">fact_check</span>
    <div className="aic-evidence-body">
      <strong>Design for graceful failure and easy correction.</strong> Google's People + AI Guidebook has a full Errors and Graceful Failure chapter; Microsoft's G9 calls for supporting efficient correction; NN/g documents that citing sources and enabling verification is what makes AI answers trustworthy.
      <span className="aic-cite">Google PAIR, Errors + Graceful Failure; Microsoft HAX G9; NN/g. Sources below.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-apply">
    <div className="aic-apply-title"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">fact_check</span> In a RamosLabs product</div>
    <p className="aic-apply-text">
      Show the sources a claim rests on as chips the user can open. Offer <strong>Report</strong> and <strong>Suggest a fix</strong> on every answer. When the system is unsure, it says so rather than filling the gap with a plausible invention.
    </p>
    <div className="aic-gen">
      <div className="aic-gen-bar">
        <span className="aic-gen-badge"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">auto_awesome</span> Generated</span>
      </div>

          <span className="aic-source"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">description</span> run-of-show.pdf</span>
          <span className="aic-source"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">description</span> venue-contract.pdf</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div className="aic-gen-foot">
        <button className="aic-btn" type="button"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">edit_note</span> Suggest a fix</button>
        <button className="aic-btn" type="button"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">flag</span> Report</button>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-line">
    <div className="aic-line-col ok">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Cite sources, let users correct or report, and admit uncertainty when the answer is thin. <strong>Every claim is checkable, and every miss is fixable.</strong></p>
    </div>
    <div className="aic-line-col avoid">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Sourceless assertions, no way to flag a wrong answer, or a confident fabrication where "I could not find that" was the honest reply. <strong>An unverifiable, uncorrectable answer is a liability wearing the mask of help.</strong></p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

{/* ============================================ */}
{/* 6. FEEDBACK                                   */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="aic-principle">
  <div className="aic-principle-head">
    <span className="aic-num">6</span>
    <h2 className="aic-principle-title">Make Feedback One Tap Away</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="aic-lede">
    The model improves only when it hears where it failed, and users trust a system more when their signal is welcome. Put a <strong>lightweight rating</strong> on every answer, thumbs up or down, with a quiet path to report something harmful. Ask, acknowledge, and never nag.
  </p>

  <div className="aic-rule">
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Rule</span> Offer a one-tap rating and a report path on every generated answer, and acknowledge the input without nagging.</p>
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Why</span> Cheap-to-give feedback improves the system and signals that the user's judgment counts.</p>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-evidence">
    <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">thumb_up</span>
    <div className="aic-evidence-body">
      <strong>Learn from user behavior and encourage granular feedback.</strong> Microsoft's guidelines G12 and G15 to G17 cover remembering, learning from, and updating on user feedback; Google's guidebook makes feedback and control a core chapter of the design process.
      <span className="aic-cite">Microsoft HAX G12, G15 to G17; Google PAIR, Feedback + Control. Sources below.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-apply">
    <div className="aic-apply-title"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">thumb_up</span> In a RamosLabs product</div>
    <p className="aic-apply-text">
      Sit thumbs and a report flag in the answer footer, low-key until hovered. A tap confirms with a quiet "Thanks, noted," never a modal. Optional detail on a down-vote, never required.

      <div className="aic-gen-bar">

      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-line">
    <div className="aic-line-col ok">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">A quiet thumbs pair and report flag, a one-line acknowledgement, optional extra detail. <strong>Giving feedback costs the user a single tap.</strong></p>

    <div className="aic-line-col avoid">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>

{/* 7. PRIVACY AND DISCLAIMERS                     */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="aic-principle">
  <div className="aic-principle-head">
    <span className="aic-num">7</span>
    <h2 className="aic-principle-title">Say What Happens to the Data</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="aic-lede">
    People share more with a system they understand. Before an input reaches a model, state <strong>what is sent, where it goes, and whether it trains anything</strong>, in plain words at the moment of entry. A disclaimer is not fine print. It is a promise placed where the user can act on it.
  </p>

  <div className="aic-rule">
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Rule</span> Disclose what data the feature sends, where it goes, and whether it trains a model, in plain language at the point of input.</p>
    <p className="aic-rule-line"><span className="aic-rule-key">Why</span> Informed consent needs the facts before the send, not buried in a policy read by no one.</p>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-evidence">
    <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">shield</span>
    <div className="aic-evidence-body">
      <strong>Be transparent about data collection and honor its limits.</strong> Google's People + AI Guidebook dedicates a Data Collection chapter to responsible, consented data use, and Apple's guidance stresses being clear and up front about how personal data feeds machine-learning features.
      <span className="aic-cite">Google PAIR, Data Collection; Apple HIG, Machine learning. Sources below.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-apply">
    <div className="aic-apply-title"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">shield</span> In a RamosLabs product</div>
    <p className="aic-apply-text">
      A one-line disclaimer sits beside the AI input, in muted <code>--color-text-body</code> that still clears AA contrast. It names the boundary in words a person can act on, with a link to the detail for anyone who wants it.
    </p>
    <div className="aic-disclaimer">
      <span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">info</span>
      <span>Your text is sent to our AI provider to generate this draft. It is not used to train their models, and it is deleted after processing. Read how we handle AI data.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="aic-line">
    <div className="aic-line-col ok">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Recommended</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Name what leaves the device and what happens to it, in plain words at the input. <strong>The user consents knowing the facts, before anything is sent.</strong></p>
    </div>
    <div className="aic-line-col avoid">
      <div className="aic-line-tag"><span className="aic-material" aria-hidden="true">cancel</span> Avoid</div>
      <p className="aic-line-text">Hiding data use in a linked policy, or quietly training on user content without a clear, visible say. <strong>Consent extracted through omission is not consent.</strong></p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="aic-ethics">
