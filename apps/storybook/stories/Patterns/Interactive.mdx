{/* 3 - THE HOVER GATE                            */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="ix-section">
  <div className="ix-section-header">
    <span className="ix-section-number">3</span>
    <h2 className="ix-section-title">Hover belongs to pointers</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="ix-text">
    Wrap every hover rule in <code>@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)</code>. It fires only where a pointer can actually rest over a control.
  </p>

  <div className="ix-callout">
    <span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">warning</span>
    <p className="ix-callout-text">
      <strong>The sticky hover bug.</strong> A touchscreen has no pointer to move away, so it never clears an ungated hover. Tap one and the style <strong>sticks</strong>: buttons stay lit, cards freeze mid transition. The gate is the fix, not a nicety.
    </p>
  </div>

{/* 4 - THE FOCUS RING                            */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="ix-section">
  <div className="ix-section-header">
    <span className="ix-section-number">4</span>
    <h2 className="ix-section-title">A ring you can always see</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="ix-text">
    Style <code>:focus-visible</code>, not <code>:focus</code>: the ring shows for the keyboard and stays hidden after a plain click. The harder half is making it survive every surface. A focus indicator has to clear <strong>3:1 against whatever sits behind it</strong>, and one flat color cannot promise that. White dissolves on a light page; indigo dissolves on an indigo button. <code>--shadow-focus</code> is two-tone for exactly this reason, a white gap inside an indigo edge, so whichever layer meets the surface, the other one still reads.
  </p>

  <div className="ix-ring-grid">
    <div className="ix-ring-surface light">
      <span className="ix-ring-surface-label">On a light surface</span>
      <div className="ix-ring-pair">
        <div className="ix-ring-slot">

          <span className="ix-ring-caption">White ring, <b>gone</b></span>
        </div>
        <div className="ix-ring-slot">
          <button className="ix-ring-btn duo" type="button" tabIndex={-1}>Two-tone</button>
          <span className="ix-ring-caption">Indigo edge <b>reads</b></span>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div className="ix-ring-surface brand">
      <span className="ix-ring-surface-label">On an indigo button</span>
      <div className="ix-ring-pair">
        <div className="ix-ring-slot">
          <button className="ix-ring-btn mono" type="button" tabIndex={-1}>One color</button>
          <span className="ix-ring-caption">Indigo ring, <b>gone</b></span>
        </div>
        <div className="ix-ring-slot">
          <button className="ix-ring-btn duo" type="button" tabIndex={-1}>Two-tone</button>
          <span className="ix-ring-caption">White gap <b>reads</b></span>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p className="ix-text" style={{ marginTop: '1.25rem' }}>
    An <code>outline</code> with <code>outline-offset</code> earns the same guarantee a different way: the offset opens a transparent gap so the page shows through, and the outline rides just outside it. Either shape works. The real mistake is <code>outline: none</code> with nothing put back, or a single-color ring that quietly fades into a matching surface.
  </p>

  <div className="ix-callout">
    <span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">verified</span>
    <p className="ix-callout-text">
      <strong>WCAG 2.2.</strong> A focus indicator has to be visible (SC 2.4.7 Focus Visible, AA) and clear <strong>3:1 against its adjacent colors</strong> (SC 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast, AA). SC 2.4.13 Focus Appearance (AAA) adds a floor: an area at least a 2px perimeter thick and a 3:1 change between the unfocused and focused states. The two-tone ring is 4px of indicator and one layer always crosses 3:1, so it clears all three on light and on brand.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div className="ix-code">
    <div className="ix-code-header">

      <span className="ix-code-dot yellow" aria-hidden="true"></span>

    <span class="property">outline</span><span class="punctuation">:</span> <span class="value">none</span><span class="punctuation">;</span>
    <span class="property">box-shadow</span><span class="punctuation">:</span> <span class="value">var(--shadow-focus)</span><span class="punctuation">;</span> <span class="comment">/* 0 0 0 2px #fff, 0 0 0 4px #4f46e5 */</span>

<div className="ix-section">
  <div className="ix-section-header">
    <span className="ix-section-number">5</span>
    <h2 className="ix-section-title">The rules</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="ix-text">
    Each rule is stated in the positive, with the reason it exists. Where the wrong turn is easy to make, a recommended and an avoid card sit side by side.
  </p>

  <div className="ix-rules">

    <div className="ix-rule">
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag rule">Rule</span>Design the press first, then the keyboard ring, then hover.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag why">Why</span>The press is the one signal every device can make, so <code>:active</code> is the floor, <code>:focus-visible</code> the next layer, and hover the pointer garnish on top. Feedback built on hover alone leaves touch with nothing.</p>
    </div>

    <div className="ix-rule">
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag rule">Rule</span>Wrap every hover rule in <code>@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)</code>.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag why">Why</span>A touchscreen has no pointer to move away, so an ungated hover sticks lit after a tap. The gate fires hover only where a pointer can rest.</p>
      <div className="ix-ra">
        <div className="ix-ra-card reco">
          <div className="ix-ra-head"><span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span>Recommended</div>
          <p className="ix-ra-body">Hover lives inside the pointer gate, so it clears the moment the finger lifts.</p>
        </div>
        <div className="ix-ra-card avoid">
          <div className="ix-ra-head"><span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">block</span>Avoid</div>
          <p className="ix-ra-body">An ungated <code>:hover</code> has no way to reset on touch, so the control freezes lit after a tap.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="ix-rule">
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag rule">Rule</span>Signal every state with the tonal indigo overlay on the <code>--state-*</code> scale.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag why">Why</span>One translucent layer expresses hover, press and drag with a single rule, and color carries the meaning on every input.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag house">House rule</span>In this design system shadow is reserved for <strong>elevation, never for state</strong>. Material 3 does raise cards and buttons on hover; RamosLabs keeps the shadow still on purpose, so one mechanism carries every state. This is a RamosLabs decision, not an M3 mandate.</p>
      <div className="ix-ra">
        <div className="ix-ra-card reco">
          <div className="ix-ra-head"><span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span>Recommended</div>
          <p className="ix-ra-body">Deepen the tonal overlay for hover and press. The card holds its elevation.</p>
        </div>
        <div className="ix-ra-card avoid">
          <div className="ix-ra-head"><span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">block</span>Avoid</div>
          <p className="ix-ra-body">Lifting a card or swapping its shadow to signal hover. Here that spends elevation on a job the overlay already does.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="ix-rule">
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag rule">Rule</span>Ring with <code>--shadow-focus</code> on <code>:focus-visible</code>, and keep the token two-tone.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag why">Why</span><code>:focus-visible</code> shows the ring for the keyboard and hides it after a plain click. A flat ring fades into a matching surface, so two tones keep one layer above 3:1 on any background.</p>
      <div className="ix-ra">
        <div className="ix-ra-card reco">
          <div className="ix-ra-head"><span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span>Recommended</div>
          <p className="ix-ra-body"><code>:focus-visible</code> with the two-tone <code>--shadow-focus</code>, so the ring reads on light and on brand.</p>
        </div>
        <div className="ix-ra-card avoid">
          <div className="ix-ra-head"><span className="ix-material" aria-hidden="true">block</span>Avoid</div>
          <p className="ix-ra-body">Styling <code>:focus</code> instead of <code>:focus-visible</code>, or a one-color ring that vanishes into a surface of the same color.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div className="ix-rule">
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag rule">Rule</span>Gate transforms behind <code>prefers-reduced-motion</code> and let color carry the state without them.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag why">Why</span>The overlay already tells the whole story, so a reduced-motion visitor loses no information when the scale or lift is dropped.</p>
    </div>

    <div className="ix-rule">
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag rule">Rule</span>Give controls a 6px radius, <code>--radius-sm</code>, and pair each visual state with its ARIA.</p>
      <p className="ix-rule-line"><span className="ix-rule-tag why">Why</span>A shared radius keeps controls in one family, and a pill radius stays on chips rather than wide buttons. Screen readers need <code>aria-busy</code>, <code>aria-disabled</code> and friends to hear what the color shows.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
