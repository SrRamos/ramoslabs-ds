<div className="ix-section">
  <div className="ix-section-header">
    <span className="ix-section-number">4</span>
    <h2 className="ix-section-title">The Golden Rule of Hover</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="ix-text">
    Hover styling belongs to devices that can actually hover. Wrap every hover rule in <code>@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)</code>. <div className="ix-section">
  <div className="ix-section-header">
    <span className="ix-section-number">9</span>
    <h2 className="ix-section-title">Rules of Implementation</h2>
  </div>

  <div className="ix-dodont">
    <div className="ix-list do">
      <div className="ix-list-head">
        <span className="ix-material" style={{ fontSize: '1.25rem' }} aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span>
        Do
      </div>
      <ul>
        <li>Design <code>:active</code> first, then <code>:focus-visible</code>, then hover.</li>
        <li>Wrap every hover in <code>@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)</code>.</li>
        <li>Express state with tonal indigo via <code>--state-*</code> opacity.</li>
        <li>Ring with <code>--shadow-focus</code> on <code>:focus-visible</code> only.</li>
        <li>Gate any transform behind <code>prefers-reduced-motion</code>.</li>
        <li>Give controls a 6px radius, <code>--radius-sm</code>.</li>
        <li>Pair every visual state with its ARIA (<code>aria-pressed</code>, <code>aria-busy</code>, <code>aria-disabled</code>).</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="ix-list dont">
      <div className="ix-list-head">
        <span className="ix-material" style={{ fontSize: '1.25rem' }} aria-hidden="true">cancel</span>
        Don't
      </div>
      <ul>
        <li>Build feedback on hover alone. Touch gets nothing.</li>
        <li>Leave hover ungated. It sticks after a tap on touch.</li>
        <li>Lift an element or swap its shadow to signal hover.</li>
        <li>Style <code>:focus</code> when you mean <code>:focus-visible</code>.</li>
        <li>Ring in <code>white</code>. It disappears off a white surface.</li>
        <li>Move layout on interaction or animate through reduced-motion.</li>
        <li>Put a pill radius on a wide button.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p className="ix-text" style={{ marginTop: '1.5rem' }}>
    <strong>Decision ladder.</strong> Adding a state to a control? Stop at the first rung that answers it.
  </p>

  <div className="ix-ladder">
    <div className="ix-ladder-step">
      <p className="ix-ladder-text"><strong>Can every input trigger it?</strong> If yes it is press or one of its cousins. Build it at the top level, ungated, so touch gets it.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="ix-ladder-step">
      <p className="ix-ladder-text"><strong>Is it keyboard orientation?</strong> Then it is <code>:focus-visible</code> with <code>--shadow-focus</code>. Never plain <code>:focus</code>.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="ix-ladder-step">
      <p className="ix-ladder-text"><strong>Is it a pointer-only nicety?</strong> Then it is hover, and it lives inside the <code>@media (hover: hover)</code> gate. Nowhere else.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="ix-ladder-step">
      <p className="ix-ladder-text"><strong>Does it move?</strong> Gate the motion behind <code>prefers-reduced-motion</code> and make sure color still carries the meaning without it.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="ix-ladder-step">
      <p className="ix-ladder-text"><strong>Is it a held state?</strong> Selected, expanded, checked. Persist it and mirror it in ARIA. It is not a transient hover.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

    <strong>How this differs from a utility framework.</strong> Tailwind and Bulma hand you the hooks (<code>hover:</code>, <code>active:</code>, <code>focus:</code>) but no doctrine about when each one is right. The gate, the modality model, the state-layer scale and the accessibility floor are left to you, and left out they usually stay out.
