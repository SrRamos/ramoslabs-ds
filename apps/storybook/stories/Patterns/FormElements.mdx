          <td><code>type="search"</code></td>
          <td>Text with a Search / Go return key</td>
          <td>None</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </div>

  <div className="fe-callout">
    <span className="fe-material" aria-hidden="true">smartphone</span>
    <p className="fe-callout-text">
      <strong>Reach for the native control first.</strong> <code>type="date"</code>, <code>type="time"</code> and a real <code>&lt;select&gt;</code> already ship a picker, keyboard behaviour and accessibility that a hand-built widget has to re-earn from scratch. Build custom only when the native one truly cannot express what you need.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

{/* ============================================ */}
{/* 3: VALIDATION                                 */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="fe-section">
  <div className="fe-section-header">
    <span className="fe-section-number">3</span>
    <h2 className="fe-section-title">Validate at the right moment, in the right words</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="fe-text">
    Validation asks two things: <strong>when</strong> to check, and <strong>what</strong> to say. The rule is <strong>reward early, punish late</strong>. Confirm a field as valid while the user types, but hold the error until they leave it. Flagging the first letter of an email as a broken email is technically true and deeply unhelpful.
  </p>

  <div className="fe-timeline">
    <div className="fe-tl-step">
      <span className="fe-tl-when"><span className="fe-material" style={{ fontSize: '0.85rem' }} aria-hidden="true">keyboard</span> While typing</span>
      <div className="fe-tl-title">Reward, never punish</div>
      <div className="fe-tl-body">Show a success tick the moment input becomes valid. Hold every error back. An incomplete field is not yet a wrong one.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-tl-step">
      <span className="fe-tl-when"><span className="fe-material" style={{ fontSize: '0.85rem' }} aria-hidden="true">logout</span> On blur</span>
      <div className="fe-tl-title">Now you may correct</div>
      <div className="fe-tl-body">When focus leaves the field, the answer is final enough to judge. Surface the inline error, right under the field.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-tl-step">
      <span className="fe-tl-when"><span className="fe-material" style={{ fontSize: '0.85rem' }} aria-hidden="true">send</span> On submit</span>
      <div className="fe-tl-title">Catch the rest</div>
      <div className="fe-tl-body">Re-check everything, move focus to the first error, summarise. Never let a form fail silently.</div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p className="fe-text">
    A good error message is <strong>visible and specific</strong>, says <strong>what went wrong in plain language</strong>, and tells the user <strong>how to fix it</strong>. "Invalid input" fails all three. "Enter a date after today, the event cannot start in the past" passes all three. WCAG 3.3.1 requires the error to be identified in text, so never lean on a red border alone. The colour reinforces; the words are the message.
  </p>

  <div className="fe-lab">
    <div className="fe-demo">
      <div className="fe-demo-label">Reward: valid while typing</div>
      <div className="fe-field">
        <label className="fe-label" htmlFor="fe-ok">Email address</label>
        <input id="fe-ok" className="fe-input success" type="email" defaultValue="ana@ramoslabs.co" aria-describedby="fe-ok-msg" />
        <span id="fe-ok-msg" className="fe-success-text"><span className="fe-material" aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span> Looks good.</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-demo">
      <div className="fe-demo-label">Punish late: actionable error on blur</div>
      <div className="fe-field">
        <label className="fe-label" htmlFor="fe-bad">Email address</label>
        <input id="fe-bad" className="fe-input error" type="email" defaultValue="ana@ramoslabs" aria-invalid="true" aria-describedby="fe-bad-msg" />
        <span id="fe-bad-msg" className="fe-error"><span className="fe-material" aria-hidden="true">error</span> Add the domain ending, like .co or .com, so the address can receive mail.</span>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="fe-callout">
    <span className="fe-material" aria-hidden="true">palette</span>
    <p className="fe-callout-text">
      <strong>Errors use <code>--color-error</code>, the only warm colour on the page.</strong> Everything interactive stays indigo; red's single job is to mark a genuine problem. Do not tint help text, borders or icons red for emphasis. Reserve it, so its appearance always means something is wrong.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

{/* ============================================ */}
{/* 4: FIELD STATES                               */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="fe-section">
  <div className="fe-section-header">
    <span className="fe-section-number">4</span>
    <h2 className="fe-section-title">Every field has states</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="fe-text">
    Default, focused, filled, sometimes disabled or in error. The field inherits the system state model rather than inventing its own: focus is the indigo ring token that stays visible on any surface, and the press and hover of a checkbox or toggle are tonal indigo layers gated to devices that can produce them.
  </p>

  <div className="fe-lab">
    <div className="fe-demo">
      <div className="fe-demo-label">Input states</div>
      <div className="fe-field">
        <label className="fe-label" htmlFor="fe-s1">Default</label>
        <input id="fe-s1" className="fe-input" type="text" placeholder="Tap or click to focus" />
      </div>
      <div className="fe-field">
        <label className="fe-label">Disabled</label>
        <input className="fe-input" type="text" defaultValue="Locked while saving" disabled />
      </div>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-demo">
      <div className="fe-demo-label">Selection controls</div>
      <div className="fe-controls">

        </div>

  <p className="fe-text">
    Look at the defaults: the reminder is pre-checked because people want it, the newsletter is left off because it costs their attention. A default must be the choice the user would make with full attention, and it must stay editable. Pre-fill to save effort, never to extract a choice.
  </p>

<div className="fe-section">
  <div className="fe-section-header">
    <span className="fe-section-number">5</span>
    <h2 className="fe-section-title">A form is only done when it is accessible</h2>
  </div>

  <p className="fe-text">
    An accessible form is the same form built correctly. Three WCAG criteria carry most of it, and each maps straight to a habit from the chapters above.
  </p>

  <div className="fe-table-scroll">
    <table className="fe-table">
      <thead>
        <tr>
          <th>Criterion</th>

        </tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td className="fe-kbd">3.3.2 Labels or Instructions</td>
          <td>A</td>
          <td>Every control has a visible label or instruction.</td>

        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td className="fe-kbd">3.3.1 Error Identification</td>
          <td>A</td>

        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td className="fe-kbd">3.3.3 Error Suggestion</td>
          <td>AA</td>

This is the accessible default, not optional polish.
    </p>

{/* 6: RULES                                      */}
{/* ============================================ */}

<div className="fe-section">
  <div className="fe-section-header">
    <span className="fe-section-number">6</span>
    <h2 className="fe-section-title">Rules of the form</h2>
  </div>

  <div className="fe-dodont">
    <div className="fe-list do">
      <div className="fe-list-head">
        <span className="fe-material" style={{ fontSize: '1.25rem' }} aria-hidden="true">check_circle</span>
        Do
      </div>
      <ul>
        <li>Keep the label visible above every field, always.</li>
        <li>One field per line, a single column top to bottom.</li>
        <li>Match <code>type</code> and <code>inputmode</code> to the answer.</li>
        <li>Add the <code>autocomplete</code> token so the browser can fill it.</li>
        <li>Reward valid input while typing, surface errors on blur.</li>
        <li>Write errors that say how to fix the problem, in text.</li>
        <li>Use <code>--color-error</code> for errors and nothing else.</li>
        <li>Reach for the native control before building a custom one.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-list dont">
      <div className="fe-list-head">
        <span className="fe-material" style={{ fontSize: '1.25rem' }} aria-hidden="true">cancel</span>
        Don't
      </div>
      <ul>
        <li>Use a placeholder as the label. It vanishes on the first keystroke.</li>
        <li>Put two fields side by side just to look compact.</li>
        <li>Leave everything <code>type="text"</code> and force a keyboard hunt.</li>
        <li>Validate on every keystroke and flag half-typed input as wrong.</li>
        <li>Ship "Invalid" or "Error" with no way to recover.</li>
        <li>Signal an error with a red border and no words.</li>
        <li>Tint unrelated UI red, or add any second accent colour.</li>
        <li>Rebuild a date picker or select the platform already ships.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p className="fe-text" style={{ marginTop: '1.5rem' }}>
    <strong>Decision ladder.</strong> Adding a field? Stop at the first rung that resolves it.
  </p>

  <div className="fe-ladder">
    <div className="fe-ladder-step">
      <p className="fe-ladder-text"><strong>Does this field need to exist?</strong> Every field is a cost. If you can infer it, default it, or drop it, do that before styling anything.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-ladder-step">
      <p className="fe-ladder-text"><strong>Can a native control answer it?</strong> A <code>&lt;select&gt;</code>, <code>type="date"</code>, a checkbox. Use it, and keyboard and accessibility come built in.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-ladder-step">
      <p className="fe-ladder-text"><strong>What is the answer's shape?</strong> Pick the <code>type</code> and <code>inputmode</code> that raise the matching keyboard, and add the <code>autocomplete</code> token.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-ladder-step">
      <p className="fe-ladder-text"><strong>What can go wrong, and how is it fixed?</strong> Write the help and the recovery message before the layout. If you cannot say how to fix it, the rule is wrong, not the user.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="fe-ladder-step">
      <p className="fe-ladder-text"><strong>Only now, assemble it</strong> from the primitives: visible label, control, help, error, in one column, states inherited from Patterns / Interactive.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
